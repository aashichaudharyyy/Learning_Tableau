<h2 style="color:#3B82F6;">What is Tableau?</h2>

<p>
Tableau is a Business Intelligence and data visualization tool that transforms raw data into meaningful, interactive dashboards. 
It allows users to explore complex datasets visually and derive insights without extensive programming knowledge.
</p>

<p>
By using a drag-and-drop interface, Tableau simplifies data analysis and enables professionals to convert numerical information into clear visual stories that support decision-making.
</p>

<hr>

<h2 style="color:#7C3AED;">Why Do We Use Tableau?</h2>

<p>
In data-driven environments, organizations must interpret large volumes of information quickly and accurately. Tableau enhances this process by enabling real-time analysis, interactive filtering, and dynamic reporting.
</p>

<p>
Its ability to connect to multiple data sources and generate dashboards efficiently makes it a powerful tool for analytics and business intelligence.
</p>

<hr>

<h2 style="color:#DC2626;">Why Tableau Over Other Tools?</h2>

<p>
While spreadsheet tools and programming languages can perform data analysis, Tableau provides a balance between analytical capability and usability. It handles larger datasets than traditional spreadsheets and requires significantly less coding than programming environments.
</p>

<p>
Its visualization engine and intuitive design contribute to its widespread industry adoption.
</p>

<hr>

<h2 style="color:#059669;">Disadvantages of Tableau</h2>

<p>
Despite its advantages, Tableau has limitations. Licensing costs may be high for some users, and it is primarily focused on visualization rather than deep data preprocessing or advanced algorithm development.
</p>

<p>
Performance may also depend on system resources when handling extremely large datasets.
</p>

<hr>

<h2 style="color:#F59E0B;">Use Cases of Tableau</h2>

<p>
Tableau is widely used in business analytics, finance, healthcare, education, and retail sectors. It supports tasks such as revenue analysis, performance tracking, trend monitoring, and decision-making through visual dashboards.
</p>

<hr>

<h2 style="color:#374151;">Summary</h2>

<p>
Tableau is a Business Intelligence and visualization tool that converts raw data into interactive dashboards. It is preferred for its ease of use, strong visualization capabilities, and efficiency in handling structured data for decision-making purposes.
</p>
